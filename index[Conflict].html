About me

Summary / Full
An experienced frontend web developer with strong skills in HTML5, CSS3 and Javascript. I have worked for Accenture from 2007 to present on projects at several leading international companies accross the mobile communication, new media, arts and government sectors

Whilst achieving a 1st in Maths and Physics at the University of Birmingham, I began development during these courses on languages such as Fortran and matlab. From here, I moved to development in a LAMP environment working for free on a community web project.

With this experience behind me I joined Accenture in 2007 and began work for a year at an international new media client on a pan-European web content management consolidation project. Development was in TCL and the usual frontend languages and I quickly gained experience of working in an agile development environment. Sprints lasted two weeks and production deployments worked on the same timescale, ensuring that I have a full understanding of the importance of production ready code I also learned how important the relationship between development and test teams is.

Following that, I spent two years as the lead developer on a world leading mobile communications company delivering a bespoke AJAX based order management system. The application made heavy use of Javascript using a framework called TIBCO GI. The emphasis on this project was on ensuring a good design was prepared early on to ensure downstream dependencies could be planned around. Here I became familar with how to manage work to satisy the dependencies on larger, slower, downstream teams.

A few other smaller projects followed as a team lead and a industry analyst. I have spent a large amount of time focusing heavily on performance enhancements for websites during these projects. Using tools such as fiddler, JMeter and firebug, load analysis and reduction was key to ensure that a previously very heavy loading web apps became much more responsive and usable for the average user.

Currently within Accenture, I have moved on to another long term project. Working as the frontend architect on a very large government project has provided interesting challenges. Focus here has been very much on accessibility and responsive design. I have learned a large amount about the art of responsive design and CSS3 functionality from working with Andy Clarke, who was hired given the nature of the project and it's target audience. Additionally, I have gained a full understanding of accessibility concerns within a website and those offered by the latest HTML5 features as these is a key requirement given the typical user of the site - much emphasis was placed on ensuring heavy, enterprise applications were cleaned and stramlined to make sure that the mark-up remains simple and using the best features of HTML5.
